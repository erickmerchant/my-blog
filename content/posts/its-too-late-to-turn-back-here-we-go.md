Twenty years ago this May I had just graduated with a degree in web design. My
mind was abuzz with all things xhtml, javascript, and css. I had quickly lined
up a few interviews, but disappointingly things were looking like they had the
first time I had graduated. No one seemed to want the skills I was peddling. At
the same time, a year before, my best friend had moved to Portland Oregon
chasing his music career dreams. It was a place where bands were made. I
remember working in the computer lab at my college and browsing through
craigslist, and it was just web developer, web developer, and web developer
wanted. There were so many jobs there for what I had just gotten a degree in. So
it was, that suit in hand that June, I found myself walking out onto that iconic
airport carpet at PDX. What followed was thirteen years of solid employment.

Now twenty years later I have just been laid off again for the second time in 2
years. The job boards look very grim. But I find myself preparing for a move to
Portland. It's hard not to feel hopeful when it was this same move from which my
entire career hangs. These days my home is where ever my wife, daughter, and dog
are, but after that, if I have to name a place that's home, it has to be
Portland. It's a place of hope for me.

I'll be blogging hopefully a bit as I cross the country with a car loaded up
with stuff. Subscribe to my rss to catch that.
